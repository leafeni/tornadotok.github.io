How to Spot a Storm

Learning to recognize storms can save lives. Here are key indicators:

  • On radar: Look for hook echoes, bow echoes, or strong red-green velocity couplets.
  • In the sky: Watch for rotating wall clouds, shelf clouds, or greenish sky colors.
  • Other signs: Sudden drop in temperature, calm before strong winds, hail cores.

What is CAPE?

Convective Available Potential Energy measures the amount of energy for thunderstorms. High CAPE = stronger updrafts.

Understanding SRH

Storm-relative helicity describes the potential for rotating updrafts. High SRH combined with CAPE increases tornado risk.
